Highnoon – CT Guided Biopsy Navigation Device:

HighNoon is India's first shadow-based, CT-guided instrument positioning assistant
It is designed to aid clinicians in performing high-risk and high-precision percutaneous medical interventions.sss

  • The accuracy of needle placement in CT-guided interventions is highly dependent on the physician's skill and experience.
  • Vulnerable anatomy near the target area is susceptible to injury.
  • Conventional techniques often require multiple needle adjustments and intra-procedural imaging
  • This leads to longer procedures
  • It also increases patient pain
  • Radiation exposure is increased
  • Procedural risk is increased
  • HighNoon offers complete procedural control through a user-friendly planning station.
  • It provides simple, shadow-based, real-time guidance.
  • It allows for confirmation of instrument placement.  
  • It helps users target lesions of any size and location.
  • HighNoon aims to improve efficacy by enabling accurate instrument placement.
  • It minimizes iterations.
  • It reduces patient puncture and radiation.
  • It promotes quicker procedures.
  • It allows for better scanner utilization.
  • It improves patient and clinician comfort.
  • HighNoon employs a three-level confirmation process to ensure needle point accuracy
  • The first level is laser crosshair alignment to the center of the needle hub.
  • The second level is the alignment of all 4 needle shadows to the laser crosshair.
  • The third level is the alignment of the needle hub to the center of the green circle on the monitor.
  • Clinical data shows HighNoon achieves 2mm positioning accuracy.
  • It has a 3% manipulation rate.
  • The average time to place the needle is 11 minutes.
  • The average wheel-in and wheel-out time is 26 minutes.
  • These averages are calculated from 55 patient data points.
